About me

Hi, my name is Radi. I'm newly hatched web developer trying to find his place in the IT jungle. For the last two years I was playing around with Java, Python an Pythons's framework Django, Bootstrap, Javascript, jQuery, Ajax, Ruby and Ruby on Rails. After playing around with every one of these I'm kinda settled with Ruby and Rails at the moment. Of course I'm still using Boostrap, Javascript and it's libraries on a daily basis, but when in comes to the back-end stuff Ruby and Rails are my tools.

My journey towards becoming a developer began in the end of 2013. Having some knowledge about HTML and CSS I've done a quick refresh at Codeacademy. After that, I went through Michael Hartl's book. Next I completed a few courses on Udacity and then enrolled for "Intro to programming" nanodegree. I wrapped that up for two months and continued following online tutorials. To name a few: Tango with Django, Django Girls, Marina Mele's site and quite a few from Coding for Entrepreneurs. At some point I decided that working with Django is somewhat tedious and decided to go back to Rails. I started The Odin Project and completed Web 101, Ruby on Rails and half the Ruby section. After reaching to the jQuery, Javascript and front-end section I decided to jump to Freecodecamp, because of their extensive list of challenges.

In my free time I love to travel, read books, spend time hanging with interesting people, hiking, doing electronics DIY projects. I'm an AOW diver and love the company of the sharks, mantas and other sea creatures big or small. Also I hope this summer (2016) I'll get my paragliding certificate.
